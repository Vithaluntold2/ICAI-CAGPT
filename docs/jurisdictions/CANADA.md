## 📋 Regulatory Bodies

### Federal Tax Authority
**Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)**
- Administers: Federal income tax, GST/HST, payroll
- Website: canada.ca/en/revenue-agency.html
- Helpline: 1-800-959-8281 (individuals), 1-800-959-5525 (businesses)
- My Account: Online portal for individuals
- My Business Account: Online portal for businesses

### Accounting & Audit Bodies
**CPA Canada (Chartered Professional Accountants of Canada)**
- National organization (unified in 2013)
- Replaced: CA, CGA, CMA designations
- Website: cpacanada.ca
- Members: 220,000+ CPAs

**Provincial CPA Bodies** (13 total):
- Each province/territory has its own CPA body
- Examples: CPA Ontario, CPA Alberta, CPA British Columbia
- License CPAs at provincial level

**Canadian Public Accountability Board (CPAB)**
- Oversees: Audits of public companies
- Similar to: US PCAOB
- Website: cpab-ccrc.ca

**Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA)**
- Umbrella organization of provincial securities regulators
- No federal securities regulator (unlike SEC in US)
- Website: securities-administrators.ca

## 📊 Accounting Standards

### International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)
**Mandatory for**: Publicly accountable enterprises (since 2011)  
**Applies to**: Public companies, rate-regulated entities, financial institutions  
**Maintained by**: IASB (International Accounting Standards Board)  
**Adoption**: Full IFRS as issued by IASB

**Key Standards**:
- **IFRS 15**: Revenue from Contracts with Customers
- **IFRS 16**: Leases
- **IFRS 9**: Financial Instruments
- **IAS 1**: Presentation of Financial Statements
- **IAS 16**: Property, Plant and Equipment

### Accounting Standards for Private Enterprises (ASPE)
**Optional for**: Private companies (can choose IFRS or ASPE)  
**Developed by**: CPA Canada Accounting Standards Board (AcSB)  
**Advantage**: Simpler than IFRS, cost-benefit focused

**Key Sections**:
- **Section 1000**: Financial Statement Concepts
- **Section 1400**: General Standards of Financial Statement Presentation
- **Section 3031**: Inventories
- **Section 3061**: Property, Plant and Equipment
- **Section 3856**: Financial Instruments

**Major ASPE vs IFRS Differences**:
- Simplified financial instrument accounting
- No impairment reversal for certain assets
- Different goodwill impairment testing
- Simpler employee benefits accounting

### Accounting Standards for Not-for-Profit Organizations (ASNPO)
**Applies to**: Nonprofit organizations  
**Sections**: Part III of CPA Canada Handbook

## 💼 Business Structures

### 1. Sole Proprietorship
- **Registration**: Business name registration (varies by province)
- **Tax ID**: Social Insurance Number (SIN) or Business Number (BN)
- **Liability**: Unlimited personal liability
- **Taxation**: Reported on personal T1 return (business income)
- **HST/GST**: Register if revenue > $30,000

### 2. Partnership
**Types**:
- **General Partnership**: All partners have unlimited liability
- **Limited Partnership (LP)**: At least one general partner with unlimited liability
- **Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)**: Available for professionals (restricted in some provinces)

**Registration**: Partnership agreement + provincial registration  
**Tax**: Pass-through (Form T5013, partners report on T1)  
**Liability**: Varies by type  
**Provincial Variations**: LLP rules differ by province

### 3. Corporation
**Types**:
- **Federal Corporation**: Incorporated under Canada Business Corporations Act (CBCA)
- **Provincial Corporation**: Incorporated under provincial act (e.g., Ontario Business Corporations Act)

**Corporation Number**: Issued upon incorporation  
**Business Number (BN)**: 9-digit number from CRA (for tax accounts)

#### Private Corporation (Private Company)
- **Canadian-Controlled Private Corporation (CCPC)**: Significant tax advantages
- **Shareholders**: Typically < 50, restrictions on share transfer
- **Tax Rate**: Lower small business rate on first $500K of active business income
- **Directors**: Minimum 1 (25% must be Canadian residents for federal corp)

#### Public Corporation
- **Listing**: Shares traded on stock exchange
- **Securities Regulation**: Provincial (no federal regulator)
- **Reporting**: Quarterly and annual filings with securities commissions
- **Tax Rate**: General corporate rate (no small business deduction)

### 4. Unlimited Liability Company (ULC)
- **Available in**: Alberta, British Columbia, Nova Scotia
- **Feature**: Unlimited liability but flow-through tax treatment for US owners
- **Use**: Cross-border tax planning

## 💰 Tax System

### Federal Income Tax (Individuals)

#### Tax Rates (2024)
| Taxable Income | Federal Rate |
|----------------|--------------|
| Up to $55,867 | 15% |
| $55,868 - $111,733 | 20.5% |
| $111,734 - $173,205 | 26% |
| $173,206 - $246,752 | 29% |
| Over $246,752 | 33% |

**Basic Personal Amount (2024)**: $15,705 (indexed annually)

#### Provincial/Territorial Tax
**Separate from federal**: Each province/territory has own rates  
**Combined Marginal Rates** (top bracket, 2024 estimates):
- **Highest**: Nova Scotia (~54%), Newfoundland and Labrador (~51.3%)
- **Lowest**: Alberta (~48%), Nunavut (~44.5%)
- **Ontario**: ~53.53%
- **Quebec**: ~53.31% (Quebec administers own tax system)

**Quebec**: Only province with separate tax return (federal + provincial)

#### Capital Gains
**Inclusion Rate** (2024 changes):
- **For individuals**: 50% for first $250,000 of gains, 66.7% for gains above $250,000
- **For corporations**: 66.7% on all capital gains
- **Principal Residence Exemption**: Full exemption for primary residence

**Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption (LCGE)**:
- Qualified small business corporation shares: $1,016,836 (2024, indexed)
- Qualified farm/fishing property: $1,016,836

### Corporate Income Tax

#### Federal Rates (2024)
- **General Rate**: 15% (after federal abatement)
- **Small Business Deduction**: 9% on first $500,000 active business income (for CCPCs)
- **Manufacturing & Processing Deduction**: 13%

#### Provincial/Territorial Rates (2024 estimates)
**Combined Federal + Provincial**:
- **Alberta**: 23% (11% general rate, 2% small business)
- **Ontario**: 26.5% (11.5% general, 3.2% small business)
- **British Columbia**: 27% (12% general, 2% small business)
- **Quebec**: 26.5% (11.5% general, 3.2% small business) - separate administration

**CCPC Small Business Rate**: As low as 11% combined (federal + provincial)

#### Integration of Corporate and Personal Tax
**Dividend Tax Credit**: Designed to prevent double taxation  
**Types**:
- **Eligible Dividends**: From public companies or general rate income (enhanced credit)
- **Non-eligible Dividends**: From small business income (regular credit)

**Gross-up and Credit Mechanism**: Individuals gross up dividends and claim credit

### Goods and Services Tax / Harmonized Sales Tax (GST/HST)

#### GST (Federal) - 5%
**Applies in**:
- Alberta
- British Columbia (+ 7% PST)
- Manitoba (+ 7% PST)
- Northwest Territories
- Nunavut
- Quebec (+ 9.975% QST)
- Saskatchewan (+ 6% PST)
- Yukon

#### HST (Harmonized)
**Province-specific combined rates**:
- **Ontario**: 13% (5% federal + 8% provincial)
- **New Brunswick**: 15% (5% + 10%)
- **Newfoundland and Labrador**: 15% (5% + 10%)
- **Nova Scotia**: 15% (5% + 10%)
- **Prince Edward Island**: 15% (5% + 10%)

#### Registration
**Threshold**: $30,000 in taxable supplies over 4 consecutive calendar quarters  
**Small Suppliers**: Exempt if below threshold (but can voluntarily register)  
**GST/HST Number**: 9-digit BN + RT0001 (e.g., 123456789RT0001)

#### Filing Frequency
- **Annual**: Revenue ≤ $1.5M (file annually, due June 15 if Dec 31 year-end)
- **Quarterly**: Revenue $1.5M - $6M
- **Monthly**: Revenue > $6M

#### Input Tax Credits (ITCs)
- Can claim GST/HST paid on business expenses
- Personal use items not eligible
- Capital property: ITCs spread over time for certain assets

### Payroll Taxes

#### Canada Pension Plan (CPP)
- **2024 Rates**: 5.95% employer + 5.95% employee
- **Maximum Pensionable Earnings**: $68,500
- **Basic Exemption**: $3,500
- **Maximum Contribution**: $3,867.50 each (employer and employee)

**CPP2 (Enhanced CPP)**: Additional rate for earnings above Year's Maximum Pensionable Earnings

#### Employment Insurance (EI)
- **2024 Rate**: 1.66% employee, 2.32% employer (1.4x employee rate)
- **Maximum Insurable Earnings**: $63,200
- **Maximum Premium**: $1,049.12 employee, $1,468.77 employer

#### Quebec-Specific
**Quebec Pension Plan (QPP)**: Separate from CPP  
**Quebec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP)**: 0.494% employee, 0.692% employer (2024)

#### Payroll Remittances
- **Remittance Due**: 15th of month following payroll
- **Large employers**: More frequent (twice/month or 4 times/month)
- **Form T4**: Annual wage statement (due last day of February)
- **Form T4 Summary**: Summary filed with T4 slips

## 🔍 Audit Framework

### Canadian Auditing Standards (CAS)
**Based on**: International Standards on Auditing (ISAs)  
**Maintained by**: CPA Canada Auditing and Assurance Standards Board (AASB)

**Key Standards**:
- **CAS 200**: Overall Objectives of Independent Auditor
- **CAS 315**: Identifying and Assessing Risks of Material Misstatement
- **CAS 330**: Auditor's Responses to Assessed Risks
- **CAS 500**: Audit Evidence
- **CAS 700**: Forming Opinion and Reporting on Financial Statements
- **CAS 701**: Communicating Key Audit Matters in Independent Auditor's Report

### Audit Report Types
1. **Unmodified Opinion**: No material misstatements (clean opinion)
2. **Modified Opinion**:
   - **Qualified Opinion**: Material but not pervasive (Except for...)
   - **Adverse Opinion**: Material and pervasive
   - **Disclaimer of Opinion**: Unable to obtain sufficient evidence

### Review Engagements
**CSRE 2400**: Review of financial statements  
**Lower assurance than audit**: Limited assurance (vs reasonable assurance)  
**Often used by**: Private companies, not-for-profits

### Compilation Engagements
**CSRS 4200**: Compilation of financial information  
**No assurance provided**: Accountant simply prepares statements  
**Notice to Reader**: Issued instead of audit report

## 📝 Common Compliance Issues

### Top 10 Tax Issues in Canada

1. **CRA Payroll Audits**
   - Employee vs contractor misclassification
   - Taxable benefits not reported
   - Penalties: 10%-20% of amounts not remitted

2. **GST/HST ITC Claims**
   - Personal use not separated
   - Missing documentation
   - Penalty: 1% per month + interest

3. **Unreported Foreign Income**
   - Form T1135 (Foreign Income Verification): > $100,000 in cost
   - Penalties: $25/day (min $100, max $2,500)
   - Criminal prosecution possible

4. **Principal Residence Exemption**
   - Must report on sale (even if exempt)
   - Failure to report: Penalty of $8,000
   - Form T2091 or Schedule 3

5. **Late Filing Penalties**
   - Gross negligence penalty: 50% of understated tax
   - Repeated late filing: 10% of balance + 2%/month (max 20 months)

6. **Shareholder Loans (CCPC)**
   - Loan must be repaid within 1 year after corp year-end
   - Otherwise included in income (s. 15(2))
   - Exceptions for home purchase, shares, business use

7. **RRSP Over-Contributions**
   - Lifetime over-contribution limit: $2,000
   - Penalty: 1% per month on excess
   - Form T1-OVP to report

8. **Capital Gains on Property**
   - CRA tracking principal residence claims
   - Must designate principal residence on sale
   - Flipping properties: Business income (100% taxable)

9. **Income Splitting (Kiddie Tax - TOSI)**
   - Tax on Split Income: Tax at top marginal rate
   - Applies to certain dividends, capital gains from private corp
   - Complex attribution rules

10. **Quebec Tax Differences**
    - Separate return required
    - Different credits and deductions
    - RL slips instead of T slips

## 💡 Tax Planning Strategies

### For Individuals
1. **Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP)**
   - 2024 Contribution Limit: 18% of prior year income (max $31,560)
   - Tax-deductible contributions
   - Tax-deferred growth
   - Deadline: 60 days after year-end (March 1, 2025 for 2024)

2. **Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA)**
   - 2024 Contribution Limit: $7,000 (indexed annually)
   - Cumulative limit (if never contributed): $95,000 (2024)
   - Tax-free growth and withdrawals
   - No income testing for benefits

3. **First Home Savings Account (FHSA)**
   - New in 2023
   - Annual limit: $8,000, lifetime: $40,000
   - Tax-deductible like RRSP, tax-free withdrawal like TFSA
   - For first-time home buyers

4. **Income Splitting**
   - Pension income splitting: Up to 50% of eligible pension income
   - Spousal RRSP: Contribute to spouse's RRSP (use your deduction room)
   - Family caregiver amount: For dependants with disabilities

5. **Charitable Donations**
   - Federal credit: 15% on first $200, 29% (or 33% if income > $235,675) on excess
   - Provincial credit varies
   - Carry forward: 5 years

6. **Capital Gains Planning**
   - Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption (LCGE): $1,016,836 for QSBC shares
   - Principal residence exemption: Designate for each year owned
   - Superficial loss rules: 30 days before/after

### For Businesses (CCPC)
1. **Small Business Deduction**
   - 9% federal rate on first $500,000 active business income
   - Passive investment income > $50K: Reduces SBD limit (eliminated at $150K)

2. **Income Sprinkling (Post-TOSI Rules)**
   - Salary to family members (must be reasonable)
   - Dividends subject to TOSI unless pass reasonableness test
   - Planning: Document contributions to business

3. **Capital Dividend Account (CDA)**
   - Tax-free dividends from non-taxable portion of capital gains
   - 50% of capital gains goes to CDA (before 2024 changes)
   - Must file election (Form T2054) with dividend payment

4. **Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption (LCGE)**
   - Plan shares to qualify as QSBC shares (90% active assets test)
   - Purify corporation before sale (remove passive investments)
   - 24-month holding period

5. **Scientific Research & Experimental Development (SR&ED)**
   - Federal credit: 15% (or 35% for CCPCs)
   - Provincial credits vary (e.g., Quebec: 30%, Ontario: 8%)
   - Must file Form T661 within 12 months of year-end

6. **Deferral of Capital Gains**
   - Section 85 rollover: Transfer assets to corp at tax-deferred value
   - Section 86 reorganization: Share capital reorganization
   - Section 51 exchange: Share for share exchange

7. **Tax Loss Utilization**
   - Non-capital losses: Carry back 3 years, forward 20 years
   - Net capital losses: Carry back 3 years, forward indefinitely (offset capital gains only)
   - Acquisition of control: Restrictions on loss utilization

## 📊 Recent Tax Changes (2023-2025)

### 2024 Federal Budget
1. **Capital Gains Inclusion Rate Change**:
   - Individuals: 50% → 66.7% for gains > $250,000
   - Corporations: 50% → 66.7% on all gains
   - LCGE increased to ~$1.25M to offset

2. **Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) Changes**:
   - Rate increased from 15% to 20.5%
   - Exemption increased to $173,000
   - Fewer deductions/credits allowed

3. **Employee Ownership Trust (EOT)**:
   - Tax deferral for business sales to employee trusts
   - Capital gains exemption up to $10M

### Upcoming Changes
1. **Cannabis Excise Duty**: Adjustments to flat duty rates
2. **Clean Economy Investment Tax Credits**: Various credits for green investments
3. **Luxury Tax**: 10% or 20% on luxury cars, aircraft, boats (already in effect)

## 🎓 CPA Qualification Requirements

### CPA Certification Path (Post-2014)
**Prerequisites**:
- Bachelor's degree (any field)
- CPA-specific courses (if not in accounting degree):
  - Introductory/Intermediate Financial Accounting
  - Introductory/Intermediate Management Accounting
  - Finance
  - Audit and Assurance
  - Taxation
  - Information Technology

**CPA Professional Education Program (CPA PEP)**:
- Core 1, Core 2 (financial reporting, management accounting, strategy & governance, tax, audit)
- Tax, Assurance, Finance, or Performance Management Elective modules
- Common Final Examination (CFE) - 3-day exam
- Capstone 1 & 2 (practical case studies)

**Practical Experience**:
- 30 months of relevant work experience
- Must develop specified competencies
- Requires mentor and experience verification

**Provincial Registration**:
- Must register with provincial CPA body to practice

### Legacy Designations (Pre-2013)
- **CA** (Chartered Accountant): CICA
- **CGA** (Certified General Accountant): CGA-Canada
- **CMA** (Certified Management Accountant): CMA Canada
- **Unified in 2013** into CPA

## 🚩 Red Flags for AI Responses

### High-Risk Topics
1. **TOSI (Tax on Split Income)**: Complex reasonableness tests
2. **Transfer Pricing**: Arm's length pricing for related parties
3. **Foreign Affiliates**: Controlled/non-controlled foreign affiliate rules
4. **Section 85 Rollovers**: Tax-deferred transfers to corporations
5. **Estate Freezes**: Complex corporate reorganizations
6. **Subsection 245(2) GAAR**: General Anti-Avoidance Rule application
7. **Quebec Tax**: Separate tax system with unique rules

### Always Cite Specific Provisions
- **Income Tax Act Sections**: "Subsection 15(2)" or "Paragraph 20(1)(a)"
- **Regulations**: "Regulation 100"
- **CRA Folios**: "Income Tax Folio S4-F8-C1"
- **Interpretation Bulletins**: "IT-302R3" (if still applicable)

### Jurisdiction Clarifications
- **Federal vs Provincial**: Income tax (both), sales tax (varies)
- **Province-Specific**: Quebec has separate administration
- **Municipal**: Property tax, business licenses
