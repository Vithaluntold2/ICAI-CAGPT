## 📋 Regulatory Bodies

### Tax Authority
**Revenue Administration (Gelir İdaresi Başkanlığı - GİB)**
- Part of: Ministry of Treasury and Finance
- Administers: Income tax, corporate tax, VAT, special consumption tax
- Website: gib.gov.tr
- Online Portal: İnteraktif Vergi Dairesi (Interactive Tax Office)
- Helpline: 444 0 189

### Accounting & Audit Bodies
**Public Oversight, Accounting and Auditing Standards Authority (KGK)**
- **Turkish**: Kamu Gözetimi, Muhasebe ve Denetim Standartları Kurumu
- Established: 2011
- Website: kgk.gov.tr
- Oversees: Independent auditing, accounting standards, auditors
- Similar to: SEC + FASB + PCAOB (combined role)

**Union of Chambers of Certified Public Accountants of Turkey (TÜRMOB)**
- **Turkish**: Türkiye Serbest Muhasebeci Mali Müşavirler ve Yeminli Mali Müşavirler Odaları Birliği
- Members: CPA (SMMM) and Sworn-In CPA (YMM)
- Website: turmob.org.tr
- Regional chambers: 80+ local chambers across Turkey

### Capital Markets
**Capital Markets Board (SPK)**
- **Turkish**: Sermaye Piyasası Kurulu
- Website: spk.gov.tr
- Regulates: Stock exchange (Borsa Istanbul), securities markets, public offerings
- Similar to: US SEC

**Banking Regulation and Supervision Agency (BDDK)**
- **Turkish**: Bankacılık Düzenleme ve Denetleme Kurumu
- Website: bddk.org.tr
- Regulates: Banks and financial institutions

## 📊 Accounting Standards

### Turkish Financial Reporting Standards (TFRS/TMS)
**Turkish Names**:
- **TFRS**: Türkiye Finansal Raporlama Standartları (equivalent to IFRS)
- **TMS**: Türkiye Muhasebe Standartları (equivalent to IAS)

**Basis**: Adopted from International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) and IAS  
**Maintained by**: KGK (Public Oversight Authority)  
**Mandatory for**: Public companies, banks, insurance companies, large private companies (based on size criteria)

#### Adoption Status
- **IFRS Adoption**: Full adoption with Turkish translations
- **Effective from**: January 1, 2005 for public companies
- **Updates**: KGK regularly updates TFRS/TMS to align with IASB changes

**Key Standards** (TFRS/TMS equivalents):
- **TFRS 15 (IFRS 15)**: Revenue from Contracts with Customers
- **TFRS 16 (IFRS 16)**: Leases
- **TFRS 9 (IFRS 9)**: Financial Instruments
- **TMS 1 (IAS 1)**: Presentation of Financial Statements
- **TMS 2 (IAS 2)**: Inventories
- **TMS 16 (IAS 16)**: Property, Plant and Equipment
- **TMS 18 (IAS 18)**: Revenue (superseded by TFRS 15 but still referenced)

### Small and Medium-Sized Entities (SME) Standard
**Applies to**: Companies not subject to full TFRS/TMS  
**Based on**: IFRS for SMEs  
**Turkish Name**: KOBİ TFRS (Küçük ve Orta Büyüklükteki İşletmeler TFRS)

### Uniform Chart of Accounts (Tek Düzen Hesap Planı)
- **Mandatory**: For tax purposes
- **Structure**: Standardized account codes (e.g., 100-199 for current assets)
- **Used for**: Corporate tax returns and statutory books

## 💼 Business Structures

### 1. Sole Proprietorship (Şahıs Şirketi / Şahıs İşletmesi)
- **Registration**: Trade registry, Chamber of Commerce
- **Tax ID**: Individual tax number
- **Liability**: Unlimited personal liability
- **Taxation**: Income tax (graduated rates)
- **Social Security**: Mandatory (Bağ-Kur or SGK)

### 2. Ordinary Partnership (Adi Ortaklık)
- **Regulation**: Turkish Code of Obligations
- **Liability**: Joint and several liability
- **Tax**: Pass-through (partners taxed individually)
- **Not a legal entity**: Cannot own property, limited legal capacity
- **Use**: Temporary projects, consortiums

### 3. Ordinary Limited Partnership (Adi Komandit Şirket)
- **Partners**: General partners (unlimited liability) + Limited partners (liability limited to capital)
- **Management**: By general partners
- **Tax**: Pass-through or corporate (depending on structure)

### 4. Collective Company (Kolektif Şirket)
- **Rare**: Seldom used in modern Turkish business
- **Liability**: All partners have unlimited liability
- **Tax**: Corporate tax (if elected) or pass-through

### 5. Limited Liability Company (Limited Şirket / Ltd. Şti.)
**Most Common Business Structure**

- **Abbreviation**: Ltd. Şti. or Ltd.
- **Regulation**: Turkish Commercial Code (TCC) No. 6102
- **Minimum Capital**: 10,000 TRY (effective 2024, previously 50,000 TRY before regulatory change)
- **Shareholders**: Minimum 1, maximum 50
- **Liability**: Limited to capital contribution
- **Management**: Managed by director(s) appointed by shareholders
- **Shares**: Not freely transferable (shareholder approval required)
- **Audit**: Mandatory for companies above certain thresholds

**Advantages**:
- Limited liability
- Flexibility in management
- Less regulatory burden than joint-stock companies

### 6. Joint-Stock Company (Anonim Şirket / A.Ş.)
**Used for Larger Companies and Public Offerings**

- **Abbreviation**: A.Ş.
- **Minimum Capital**: 
  - **Private**: 50,000 TRY
  - **Public**: 500,000 TRY (if shares offered to public)
- **Shareholders**: Minimum 1 (as of TCC 6102)
- **Shares**: Freely transferable (unless restricted by articles)
- **Management**: Board of Directors (minimum 1 member, 3 for public companies)
- **Audit**: Mandatory (independent auditor for public companies)
- **Public Offering**: Must register with SPK (Capital Markets Board)

**Required Bodies**:
- General Assembly (shareholders)
- Board of Directors
- Statutory Auditor (if not subject to independent audit)

### 7. Cooperative (Kooperatif)
- **Purpose**: Mutual benefit of members
- **Regulation**: Cooperatives Law No. 1163
- **Examples**: Agricultural cooperatives, housing cooperatives
- **Tax**: Separate corporate tax rates (applied to limited types of income)

### 8. Branch Office (Şube)
- **Parent**: Foreign company
- **Liability**: Parent company liable
- **Tax**: Corporate tax on Turkey-sourced income
- **Registration**: Trade registry, tax office, permit from Ministry of Trade

### 9. Liaison Office (İrtibat Bürosu)
- **Purpose**: Market research, no commercial activity
- **Income**: Cannot generate revenue in Turkey
- **Tax**: Subject to limited taxes (withholding on certain payments)
- **Registration**: Ministry of Trade approval required

## 💰 Tax System

### Personal Income Tax (Gelir Vergisi)

#### Tax Rates (2024)
**Progressive Rates**:

| Taxable Income (TRY) | Rate |
|----------------------|------|
| Up to 110,000 | 15% |
| 110,001 - 230,000 | 20% |
| 230,001 - 580,000 | 27% |
| 580,001 - 3,000,000 | 35% |
| Over 3,000,000 | 40% |

**Note**: Rates and brackets adjusted annually for inflation

**Exemptions**:
- **Minimum Wage Income**: Exempt from income tax
- **Education, Health Expenses**: Certain deductions allowed

#### Withholding Tax (Stopaj)
- **Wages**: Employers withhold based on progressive rates
- **Dividends**: 10% withholding (residents)
- **Interest Income**: 0%-15% depending on maturity (bank deposits)
- **Royalties**: 20% withholding

### Corporate Income Tax (Kurumlar Vergisi)

#### Standard Rate
**2024 Rate**: 25% (previously 20% before 2024, increased to 25%)

**Note**: Rate subject to change based on economic policy

#### Temporary Tax (Geçici Vergi)
- **Filing**: Quarterly (advance corporate tax)
- **Rate**: Same as annual corporate tax rate (25%)
- **Due**: 14th day of 2nd month after quarter-end
- **Creditable**: Against annual corporate tax

#### Capital Gains
- **Corporations**: Included in taxable income (25% rate)
- **Individuals**: 
  - **Securities gains**: 10% on stock gains exceeding threshold
  - **Real estate**: 20% on gains from properties held < 5 years (exempt if held > 5 years)

#### Controlled Foreign Corporation (CFC) Rules
- Turkey has CFC rules to prevent profit shifting
- Income from CFCs may be attributed to Turkish parent

### Value Added Tax (Katma Değer Vergisi - KDV)

#### Standard Rates
| Rate | Application |
|------|-------------|
| **1%** | Basic food items, agriculture, certain newspapers |
| **10%** | Education, health, housing (rental), certain foods, textbooks |
| **20%** | Standard rate (most goods and services) |

**Note**: Rates subject to change (recent years have seen frequent adjustments)

#### Registration
**Threshold**: No minimum threshold; any business activity requires VAT registration  
**VAT Number**: Assigned by tax office (10-digit number)

#### Filing
- **Monthly**: For most businesses
- **Quarterly**: Small businesses (optional)
- **Due**: 26th day of month following reporting period (24th for electronic filing)

#### Input VAT Credit
- Businesses can credit input VAT against output VAT
- Certain expenses excluded (e.g., entertainment, some vehicle expenses)
- Refunds: Can request refund if input VAT > output VAT (subject to conditions)

### Special Consumption Tax (ÖTV - Özel Tüketim Vergisi)
**Applies to**: Luxury goods, tobacco, alcohol, petroleum, automobiles, electronics  
**Rates**: Vary widely by product category (can be >100% for luxury vehicles)  
**Levied**: At manufacturing or importation stage  
**Structure**: Specific (fixed amount per unit) or ad valorem (percentage of value)

### Real Estate Tax (Emlak Vergisi)
**Levied by**: Municipalities  
**Rates**: 0.1%-0.6% of property value (varies by municipality and property type)  
**Annual Payment**: Two installments (February and August)

### Stamp Duty (Damga Vergisi)
**Applies to**: Legal documents (contracts, promissory notes, receipts, financial statements)  
**Rates**: Vary by document type (e.g., 0.948% for loan agreements in 2024)  
**Payment**: At document execution (stamps or electronic payment)

### Banking and Insurance Transactions Tax (BSMV)
**Rate**: 5% on banking transactions, 1% on insurance premiums  
**Paid by**: Banks and insurance companies (typically passed to customers)

## 🔍 Audit Framework

### Turkish Auditing Standards (TDS/TDK)
**Turkish Names**:
- **TDS**: Türkiye Denetim Standartları (equivalent to ISAs)
- **TDK**: Türkiye Denetim Kavramsal Çerçevesi

**Basis**: Adopted from International Standards on Auditing (ISAs)  
**Maintained by**: KGK  
**Mandatory for**: Companies subject to independent audit

**Key Standards** (TDS equivalents):
- **TDS 200**: Overall Objectives of the Independent Auditor
- **TDS 315**: Identifying and Assessing Risks of Material Misstatement
- **TDS 330**: Auditor's Responses to Assessed Risks
- **TDS 500**: Audit Evidence
- **TDS 700**: Forming an Opinion and Reporting on Financial Statements
- **TDS 701**: Communicating Key Audit Matters

### Independent Audit Requirements
**Mandatory for companies meeting 2 of 3 criteria** (2024 thresholds):
- **Total assets**: > 75 million TRY
- **Annual revenue**: > 150 million TRY
- **Employees**: > 175

**Also mandatory for**:
- Public companies (A.Ş. listed on Borsa Istanbul)
- Banks, insurance companies, financial institutions
- Certain large private companies by regulation

### Audit Report Types
1. **Unqualified Opinion (Olumlu Görüş)**: Clean opinion, no material misstatements
2. **Qualified Opinion (Sınırlı Olumlu Görüş)**: Except for specific items
3. **Adverse Opinion (Olumsuz Görüş)**: Financial statements materially misstated
4. **Disclaimer of Opinion (Görüş Bildirmekten Kaçınma)**: Unable to obtain sufficient evidence

### Statutory Auditor (Denetçi)
- Required for A.Ş. not subject to independent audit
- Appointed by shareholders
- Reviews financial statements and management actions
- Less extensive than independent audit

## 📝 Common Compliance Issues

### Top 10 Tax Issues in Turkey

1. **Transfer Pricing Documentation**
   - Mandatory for related-party transactions
   - Arm's length principle
   - **Forms**: Annual Transfer Pricing Report (Yıllık Transfer Fiyatlandırması Raporu) + Country-by-Country Reporting (CbCR)
   - Penalties: Up to 3% of transaction value

2. **VAT Compliance & Invoicing**
   - E-invoicing mandatory for most businesses (e-Fatura)
   - Must issue within 7 days of transaction
   - Penalties: 1% of invoice value (VAT fraud penalties much higher)

3. **Social Security Premium Underpayment**
   - Employers must report actual wages to SGK (Social Security Institution)
   - Underreporting: Administrative fines + back premiums + penal interest
   - SGK audits common

4. **Withholding Tax on Services**
   - Must withhold on certain professional services (e.g., consultancy, engineering)
   - Rate: Typically 20%
   - Failure: Penalty + interest on underwithhelding

5. **CFC Rules & Foreign Income Reporting**
   - Turkish residents must report worldwide income
   - Controlled foreign corporations: Attribution rules
   - Foreign asset declaration: Mandatory if > certain threshold

6. **Depreciation & Deductibility**
   - Fixed asset depreciation rates set by Ministry
   - Non-deductible expenses: Entertainment (50% limit), certain vehicle costs
   - Mismatch between book and tax depreciation

7. **Late Filing & Payment Penalties**
   - **Late filing**: Fixed penalties (indexed annually, e.g., 850 TRY for 2024 for first 30 days)
   - **Late payment**: Interest (Gecikme Faizi) calculated monthly (varies, e.g., ~4% per month in 2024)
   - **Repeated late filing**: Increased penalties

8. **Special Consumption Tax (ÖTV) on Vehicles**
   - Complex rate structure based on vehicle specs
   - Importers/manufacturers must register
   - Penalties for misclassification

9. **E-Government & Digital Compliance**
   - E-invoice (e-Fatura), e-ledger (e-Defter), e-archive (e-Arşiv) mandatory for many
   - Failure to use: Fines + non-deductibility of expenses
   - Portal: GİB's e-government portal

10. **Real Estate Transactions & Valuation**
    - Property sales: VAT (if commercial) or title deed fee (if residential)
    - Valuation: Tax office may challenge transaction value
    - Undeclared rental income: Common audit target

## 💡 Tax Planning Strategies

### For Individuals
1. **Pension Contributions (BES - Bireysel Emeklilik Sistemi)**
   - Private pension contributions: Deductible up to 15% of income
   - State contribution: 25% of contributions (capped annually)
   - Tax-deferred growth

2. **Real Estate Holding Period**
   - Hold property > 5 years: Capital gains exempt
   - Primary residence: Typically exempt from capital gains

3. **Securities Investment**
   - Stock gains: 10% withholding only if > threshold (e.g., 128,000 TRY in 2024)
   - Hold in custody accounts for favorable treatment

4. **Salary vs Dividend Optimization**
   - High earners: Salary tax up to 40%
   - Dividends: 10% withholding (after 25% corporate tax)
   - Effective combined rate on dividends: ~32.5%

5. **Education & Health Deductions**
   - Certain education expenses: Deductible (limits apply)
   - Private health insurance premiums: Partially deductible

### For Businesses
1. **R&D Incentives (Ar-Ge Teşvik)**
   - **Technopark/R&D Centers**: Income tax exemption, VAT exemption on R&D equipment
   - **R&D deduction**: 100% of R&D expenses deductible (additional 100% bonus for qualifying R&D)

2. **Investment Incentives (Yatırım Teşvik)**
   - **Regional incentives**: Vary by region (eastern Turkey: highest incentives)
   - **Benefits**: VAT exemption, customs duty exemption, corporate tax reduction, social security premium support
   - **Certification**: From Ministry of Industry and Technology

3. **Free Trade Zones (Serbest Bölge)**
   - **Benefits**: VAT and customs exemption, corporate tax exemption (conditions apply)
   - **Locations**: Istanbul, Izmir, Mersin, etc.

4. **Transfer Pricing Optimization**
   - Advance Pricing Agreements (APA): Pre-approval from tax authority
   - Safe harbors: For certain low-value services
   - Documentation: Maintain contemporaneous transfer pricing docs

5. **Loss Utilization**
   - **Carry forward**: 5 years
   - **No carry back**: Turkish law does not allow loss carry-backs
   - Strategic timing of income/expenses

6. **Dividend vs Interest Planning**
   - **Interest on shareholder loans**: Deductible (thin capitalization rules apply)
   - **Dividends**: 10% withholding, not deductible
   - Debt-to-equity ratio limits

7. **Tax Treaties**
   - Turkey has 90+ double tax treaties
   - Use treaty benefits for reduced withholding rates
   - Certificate of residence required

8. **E-Export & VAT Zero-Rating**
   - Digital services exported: 0% VAT
   - Documentation: Export declaration, proof of receipt abroad

## 🌐 Digital Infrastructure

### GİB E-Government Portal (İnteraktif Vergi Dairesi)
**URL**: ivd.gib.gov.tr  
**Features**:
- File tax returns online
- View tax account, assessments, payments
- E-invoice (e-Fatura) system access
- E-ledger (e-Defter) submission
- Obtain tax documents (certificates, statements)

**Login**: 
- E-signature (e-İmza) or Mobile signature
- Tax ID + password (for individuals)

### E-Invoice (e-Fatura)
**Mandatory for**: 
- Companies above revenue threshold (threshold lowered over years, now covers most businesses)
- All public sector suppliers

**System**: 
- Electronic invoice exchange via GİB portal
- Real-time validation
- Legally equivalent to paper invoices

**Advantages**: 
- Instant delivery
- Reduced paper costs
- Automatic archiving

### E-Ledger (e-Defter)
**Required for**: E-invoice users (with exceptions)  
**Consists of**: General ledger, inventory ledger (if applicable)  
**Submission**: Annually to GİB (by specific deadline)

### E-Archive (e-Arşiv Fatura)
- Electronic invoices for customers not on e-invoice system
- Must be archived and stored electronically

### SGK (Social Security) Portal
**URL**: sgk.gov.tr  
**Functions**:
- Employer registration
- Monthly premium declarations (Aylık Prim ve Hizmet Belgesi - APH)
- View employee records
- E-signature required for declarations

### MERSIS (Central Registration System)
**URL**: mersis.gtb.gov.tr  
**Purpose**: Business registration, company information  
**Managed by**: Ministry of Trade (Union of Chambers and Commodity Exchanges of Turkey - TOBB operates the system)

## 📊 Recent Tax Changes (2023-2025)

### 2024 Tax Package (December 2023)
1. **Corporate Tax Rate Increase**: 20% → 25% (effective 2024)
2. **Income Tax Brackets**: Inflation-adjusted (annual adjustment)
3. **VAT Rates**: Adjustments for certain goods (rates on some items changed from 10% to 20%)
4. **Minimum Wage Increase**: Significant increase (impacts tax exemption, social security)

### Digital Services Tax (2020 onwards)
- **Rate**: 7.5% on digital advertising and intermediary services
- **Applies to**: Companies with global revenue > €750M and Turkey revenue > 20 million TRY
- **Targets**: Large tech companies (Google, Facebook, etc.)

### Wealth Tax Rumors
- Discussions about potential wealth tax on high-net-worth individuals
- Not yet enacted (as of 2024)

### Cryptocurrency Regulation
- 2024: Increased reporting requirements for crypto exchanges
- Tax treatment: Capital gains on crypto subject to income tax
- Regulations evolving rapidly

## 🎓 CPA Qualification Requirements

### Certified Public Accountant (SMMM - Serbest Muhasebeci Mali Müşavir)

**Educational Requirements**:
- Bachelor's degree in:
  - Business Administration
  - Economics
  - Public Finance
  - Accounting
  - Or related fields (may require additional coursework)

**Experience**:
- **3 years** of practical experience in accounting/auditing
- Must work under supervision of a licensed SMMM or YMM
- Experience verified by TÜRMOB

**Examination**:
- **SMMM Exam**: Administered by TÜRMOB (usually once per year)
- **Subjects**: 
  - Financial Accounting
  - Cost Accounting
  - Auditing
  - Tax Law
  - Commercial Law
  - Financial Management
- **Pass Rate**: Historically around 15-25%

**Registration**:
- Register with local Chamber of CPAs (SMMM Odası)
- Membership fee required

### Sworn-In CPA (YMM - Yeminli Mali Müşavir)
**Higher Qualification** (equivalent to advanced CPA/auditor)

**Requirements**:
- Must already be licensed SMMM
- **10 years** of experience as SMMM
- Pass YMM examination
- Can perform independent audits, expert witness services

**Examination**:
- More advanced than SMMM exam
- Covers auditing, forensic accounting, expert testimony
- Very competitive (low pass rate)

## 🚩 Red Flags for AI Responses

### High-Risk Topics
1. **Transfer Pricing**: Complex documentation, APA procedures
2. **Free Trade Zone Incentives**: Highly specific qualification criteria
3. **CFC Rules**: Controlled foreign corporation attribution rules
4. **E-Invoice/E-Ledger Mandates**: Rapidly evolving technical requirements
5. **Regional Investment Incentives**: Vary by province/district, change frequently
6. **Tax Treaties**: Must cite specific treaty articles and protocol
7. **Currency Transactions**: Forex gain/loss treatment, inflation adjustment

### Always Cite Specific Provisions
- **Law Number**: "Corporate Tax Law No. 5520, Article 10"
- **Circular Number**: "GİB Circular No. 123"
- **Communiqué**: "General Communiqué on Corporate Tax No. 1"
- **KGK Standards**: "TDS 700" or "TFRS 15"

### Jurisdiction Clarifications
- **Language Barrier**: Always provide Turkish term alongside English
- **Regulatory Changes**: Tax laws change frequently (especially rates, brackets)
- **Regional Variations**: Some local taxes vary by municipality
- **EU Alignment**: Turkey is aligning certain laws with EU (ongoing process)

## 🗒️ AI Training Notes

**Critical Considerations**:
- Turkey's tax and regulatory environment changes frequently (inflation, economic policy)
- Always verify rates, thresholds, and deadlines with latest official sources
- Language: Turkish is official language; all official documents in Turkish
- E-government systems mandatory for most compliance activities
- Penalty regime strict: Late filing/payment can result in significant fines
- Professional advice essential for complex matters (transfer pricing, investment incentives)

**Common Pitfalls**:
- Confusing TFRS (Turkish IFRS) with tax accounting (Uniform Chart of Accounts)
- Not distinguishing between A.Ş. (joint-stock) and Ltd. Şti. (limited liability)
- Overlooking e-invoice requirements (can result in expense disallowance)
- Ignoring social security obligations (SGK premiums, declarations)
- Underestimating complexity of investment incentives (certification, regional variations)

**Document Maintenance**:
- Update after annual Tax Package (usually December for following year)
- Monitor GİB circulars and communiqués (frequent guidance updates)
- Track inflation adjustments (brackets, thresholds, fixed penalties indexed)
- Follow KGK pronouncements for accounting/audit standard changes
