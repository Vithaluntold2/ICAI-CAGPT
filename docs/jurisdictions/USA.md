## 📊 Accounting Standards (US GAAP)

### Accounting Standards Codification (ASC)
**Structure**: Topic-Subtopic-Section-Paragraph  
**Maintained by**: FASB  
**Major Topics**:
- **ASC 606**: Revenue from Contracts with Customers
- **ASC 842**: Leases
- **ASC 326**: Credit Losses (CECL model)
- **ASC 815**: Derivatives and Hedging
- **ASC 718**: Stock Compensation
- **ASC 740**: Income Taxes
- **ASC 810**: Consolidation
- **ASC 820**: Fair Value Measurement
- **ASC 350**: Intangibles (including Goodwill)

### US GAAP vs IFRS
**Key Differences**:
- **LIFO**: Allowed in US GAAP, prohibited in IFRS
- **Reversal of impairments**: Prohibited for long-lived assets in US GAAP
- **Development costs**: Expensed in US GAAP, can be capitalized in IFRS
- **Extraordinary items**: No longer allowed in US GAAP
- **Component depreciation**: Required in IFRS, optional in US GAAP

### Financial Statement Requirements
**Public Companies (SEC filers)**:
- Form 10-K: Annual report (audited)
- Form 10-Q: Quarterly report (reviewed, not audited)
- Form 8-K: Current events
- Regulation S-X: Financial statement requirements

**Private Companies**:
- Can use simplified alternatives (Private Company Council guidance)
- No mandatory public filing (unless debt covenants require)

## 💼 Business Structures

### 1. Sole Proprietorship
- **Formation**: No formal registration (can use DBA - Doing Business As)
- **Tax ID**: Social Security Number (SSN) or EIN
- **Liability**: Unlimited personal liability
- **Taxation**: Pass-through (Schedule C on Form 1040)
- **Self-Employment Tax**: 15.3% (Social Security + Medicare)

### 2. Partnership
**Types**:
- **General Partnership (GP)**: All partners have unlimited liability
- **Limited Partnership (LP)**: At least one general partner with unlimited liability
- **Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)**: Available for professionals (law, accounting)

**Formation**: Partnership agreement (written recommended, not required)  
**Tax**: Pass-through (Form 1065, K-1 to partners)  
**Liability**: Varies by type  
**Annual Filing**: Varies by state

### 3. Limited Liability Company (LLC)
- **Formation**: File Articles of Organization with state
- **Members**: Can be individuals, corporations, other LLCs
- **Liability**: Limited to investment
- **Taxation**: Default pass-through (can elect C-corp or S-corp treatment)
- **Operating Agreement**: Governs member relationships
- **Flexibility**: Most flexible business structure
- **Annual Reports**: Required by most states

**Tax Classifications**:
- **Single-member LLC**: Disregarded entity (Schedule C)
- **Multi-member LLC**: Partnership (Form 1065)
- **LLC electing S-corp**: Form 1120-S
- **LLC electing C-corp**: Form 1120

### 4. S Corporation
- **Formation**: File Articles of Incorporation + IRS Form 2553 election
- **Shareholders**: Max 100, must be US citizens/residents
- **Stock**: One class only
- **Liability**: Limited
- **Taxation**: Pass-through (Form 1120-S, K-1 to shareholders)
- **Advantage**: Avoid self-employment tax on distributions
- **Reasonable Compensation**: Must pay owners W-2 salary for services

**Eligibility Requirements**:
- Domestic corporation
- Allowable shareholders (individuals, estates, certain trusts)
- One class of stock
- Not an ineligible corporation (e.g., bank, insurance company)

### 5. C Corporation
- **Formation**: File Articles of Incorporation with state
- **Shareholders**: Unlimited, can be foreign
- **Stock**: Multiple classes allowed
- **Liability**: Limited
- **Taxation**: Double taxation (corporate + dividend level)
- **Corporate Tax Rate**: 21% flat (after TCJA 2017)
- **Requirements**: Board of directors, corporate bylaws, annual meetings

**When to Choose**:
- Need to raise venture capital
- Plan to go public (IPO)
- Want to retain earnings
- International shareholders

### 6. Nonprofit Corporation (501(c)(3))
- **Formation**: State incorporation + IRS Form 1023
- **Tax-Exempt**: Federal and often state taxes
- **Donations**: Tax-deductible for donors
- **Requirements**: Charitable purpose, no private benefit
- **Reporting**: Form 990 (annual)
- **Governance**: Board of directors required

## 💰 Federal Tax System

### Income Tax (Individuals)

#### Tax Rates (2024)
**Single Filers**:
- $0 - $11,600: 10%
- $11,601 - $47,150: 12%
- $47,151 - $100,525: 22%
- $100,526 - $191,950: 24%
- $191,951 - $243,725: 32%
- $243,726 - $609,350: 35%
- Over $609,350: 37%

**Married Filing Jointly**:
- $0 - $23,200: 10%
- $23,201 - $94,300: 12%
- $94,301 - $201,050: 22%
- $201,051 - $383,900: 24%
- $383,901 - $487,450: 32%
- $487,451 - $731,200: 35%
- Over $731,200: 37%

**Standard Deduction (2024)**:
- Single: $14,600
- Married Filing Jointly: $29,200
- Head of Household: $21,900

#### Capital Gains Tax
**Long-term (held > 1 year)**:
- 0%: Taxable income up to $47,025 (single), $94,050 (married)
- 15%: Above thresholds up to $518,900 (single), $583,750 (married)
- 20%: Above upper thresholds

**Short-term**: Taxed as ordinary income

**Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)**: 3.8% on investment income if AGI exceeds $200K (single), $250K (married)

### Corporate Income Tax
**Federal Rate**: 21% flat (since Tax Cuts and Jobs Act 2017)

**No longer graduated rates** (pre-TCJA was 15%-35%)

**Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)**: Repealed for corporations (TCJA 2017)

### Pass-Through Deduction (Section 199A)
**Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction**:
- Up to 20% deduction on qualified business income
- Available to: Sole proprietors, S-corps, partnerships, LLCs
- Phase-out for specified service businesses at higher incomes
- Limitations based on W-2 wages and qualified property

### Self-Employment Tax
**Rate**: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)  
**Applied to**: Net earnings from self-employment  
**Social Security Wage Base (2024)**: $168,600  
**Additional Medicare Tax**: 0.9% on wages/SE income > $200K (single), $250K (married)

### Estimated Tax Payments
**Who Must Pay**: 
- Individuals expecting to owe $1,000+ after withholding
- Corporations expecting to owe $500+

**Payment Dates (2024)**:
- April 15
- June 17 (not June 15!)
- September 16
- January 15, 2025

**Safe Harbor**: Pay 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year (110% if AGI > $150K)

### Payroll Taxes (Employment Taxes)
**FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act)**:
- Social Security: 6.2% employer + 6.2% employee (wage base $168,600)
- Medicare: 1.45% employer + 1.45% employee (no wage base)

**FUTA (Federal Unemployment Tax Act)**:
- 6% on first $7,000 of wages (credit for state unemployment tax paid)
- Effective rate: 0.6% after credits

**Income Tax Withholding**: Based on Form W-4 elections

**Forms**:
- Form 941: Quarterly payroll tax return
- Form 940: Annual FUTA return
- Form W-2: Annual wage statement to employees
- Form 1099-NEC: Nonemployee compensation (contractors)

### Income Tax States
**No State Income Tax (9 states)**:
- Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire* (dividends/interest only), South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, Wyoming

**Highest Income Tax Rates (Top 5)**:
1. California: 13.3% (top bracket)
2. Hawaii: 11%
3. New York: 10.9%
4. New Jersey: 10.75%
5. District of Columbia: 10.75%

**Flat Tax States** (as of 2024):
- Colorado: 4.4%
- Illinois: 4.95%
- Indiana: 3.05%
- Michigan: 4.25%
- And others...

### Sales and Use Tax
**States with No Sales Tax (5)**:
- Alaska*, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, Oregon
- *Alaska allows local sales taxes

**State + Local Combined Rates** (2024 estimates):
- Tennessee: 9.548% (highest combined)
- Louisiana: 9.56%
- Arkansas: 9.44%
- Alabama: 9.29%
- Washington: 9.38%

**Nexus Rules**: Physical presence OR economic nexus (post-Wayfair decision)
- **Economic Nexus Threshold**: Typically $100K sales or 200 transactions

### Property Tax
- Administered by local governments (counties, cities, school districts)
- Based on assessed value × millage rate
- Average effective rate: 0.99% (varies widely by state)
- Highest: New Jersey (2.23%), Illinois (2.05%)
- Lowest: Hawaii (0.31%), Alabama (0.37%)

## 🔍 Audit Framework

### Auditing Standards

#### PCAOB Standards (Public Companies)
**Applies to**: Issuers (public companies, broker-dealers)  
**Key Standards**:
- **AS 1001**: Responsibilities and Functions of Independent Auditor
- **AS 2110**: Identifying and Assessing Risks of Material Misstatement
- **AS 2301**: Auditor's Responses to Risks of Material Misstatement
- **AS 2810**: Evaluating Audit Results
- **AS 3101**: The Auditor's Report

#### AICPA Standards (Private Companies)
**Statements on Auditing Standards (SAS)**:
- **AU-C 200**: Overall Objectives of Independent Auditor
- **AU-C 315**: Understanding Entity and Assessing Risks
- **AU-C 330**: Performing Audit Procedures
- **AU-C 500**: Audit Evidence
- **AU-C 700**: Forming Opinion and Reporting

### Audit Report Types
1. **Unmodified Opinion** (Clean opinion): No material misstatements
2. **Qualified Opinion**: Material but not pervasive (Except for...)
3. **Adverse Opinion**: Material and pervasive misstatements
4. **Disclaimer of Opinion**: Unable to obtain sufficient evidence

### Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) Compliance
**Applies to**: Public companies  
**Key Requirements**:
- **Section 302**: CEO/CFO certification of financial statements
- **Section 404**: Management assessment of internal controls
- **Section 409**: Real-time disclosure of material changes
- **Section 802**: Criminal penalties for document destruction

**Compliance Costs**: $1-5M+ annually (depending on company size)

### SSAE 18 / SOC Reports
**Types**:
- **SOC 1**: Controls relevant to financial reporting
- **SOC 2**: Security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, privacy
- **SOC 3**: General-use SOC 2 report

**Types of Reports**:
- **Type I**: Design of controls at a point in time
- **Type II**: Operating effectiveness over a period (6-12 months)

## 📝 Common Compliance Issues

### Top 10 Tax Issues

1. **Misclassification of Workers**
   - Employee vs Independent Contractor (Form W-2 vs 1099-NEC)
   - IRS 20-factor test, common law test
   - Penalties: Back taxes + interest + penalties

2. **Payroll Tax Noncompliance**
   - Late deposits: 2%-15% penalty
   - Failure to file Form 941: 5% per month (max 25%)
   - Trust fund recovery penalty: 100% on responsible persons

3. **Estimated Tax Underpayment**
   - Penalty: Varies (currently ~8% annually)
   - Safe harbor: 90% current year or 100%/110% prior year

4. **Basis Reporting**
   - Incorrect cost basis on securities sales
   - Form 1099-B reconciliation
   - Wash sale rules

5. **Foreign Account Reporting**
   - **FBAR** (FinCEN Form 114): Foreign accounts > $10K aggregate
   - **FATCA** (Form 8938): Specified foreign financial assets
   - Penalties: Criminal charges possible

6. **Cryptocurrency Taxation**
   - Treated as property, not currency
   - Every transaction is taxable event
   - Form 1040 yes/no question (failure to answer = perjury risk)

7. **Home Office Deduction**
   - Regular and exclusive use required
   - Simplified method: $5/sq ft (max 300 sq ft = $1,500)
   - Actual method: Calculate percentage of home

8. **State Nexus**
   - Physical presence OR economic nexus
   - Sales tax collection obligations
   - Income tax filing requirements

9. **Bonus Depreciation & Section 179**
   - Bonus depreciation: 60% in 2024 (phasing out)
   - Section 179: Up to $1.22M deduction (2024), phase-out at $3.05M
   - Applies to qualified property

10. **Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction**
    - Complex calculations
    - Specified service trade or business (SSTB) limitations
    - W-2 wage and property basis limitations

## 💡 Tax Planning Strategies

### For Individuals
1. **Retirement Contributions**
   - 401(k): $23,000 (2024) + $7,500 catch-up (age 50+)
   - IRA: $7,000 (2024) + $1,000 catch-up
   - SEP IRA: Up to 25% of compensation (max $69,000)
   - Solo 401(k): $69,000 (2024) + catch-up

2. **Tax-Loss Harvesting**
   - Offset capital gains with losses
   - $3,000 loss deduction against ordinary income
   - Wash sale rule: 30 days before/after

3. **Charitable Contributions**
   - Up to 60% of AGI for cash (with itemization)
   - Appreciated securities: Avoid capital gains + get deduction
   - Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCD): From IRA at age 70½+

4. **Health Savings Account (HSA)**
   - Triple tax advantage (deductible, grows tax-free, withdrawals tax-free for medical)
   - 2024 limits: $4,150 (individual), $8,300 (family) + $1,000 catch-up (55+)
   - High-deductible health plan required

5. **529 College Savings Plans**
   - State tax deduction (varies by state)
   - Tax-free growth for qualified education expenses
   - Can use up to $10K/year for K-12 tuition

6. **Bunching Deductions**
   - Concentrate itemized deductions into alternate years
   - Helps when close to standard deduction threshold

### For Businesses
1. **Entity Selection**
   - C-corp vs S-corp vs LLC analysis
   - Consider state taxes, self-employment tax, QBI deduction

2. **Depreciation Strategies**
   - Bonus depreciation (60% in 2024)
   - Section 179 expensing
   - Cost segregation studies for real estate

3. **R&D Tax Credit**
   - Federal credit: 20% of qualified research expenses over base amount
   - Startups: Can offset payroll tax (up to $500K)
   - State R&D credits also available

4. **Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC)**
   - Hiring from targeted groups (veterans, ex-felons, SNAP recipients)
   - Up to $9,600 per eligible employee

5. **Qualified Small Business Stock (QSBS)**
   - Section 1202: Exclude up to 100% of gain on sale
   - Must hold 5+ years
   - Must be C-corp with < $50M assets at issuance

6. **Like-Kind Exchange (1031 Exchange)**
   - Defer capital gains on real estate
   - Must be investment or business property
   - 45-day identification, 180-day completion rules

7. **Opportunity Zones**
   - Invest capital gains in Qualified Opportunity Funds
   - Defer and potentially reduce capital gains taxes
   - Hold 10+ years: Exclude gain on appreciation

## 📊 Recent Tax Law Changes (2023-2025)

### SECURE 2.0 Act (2023)
1. **RMD Age**: Increased to 73 (will be 75 in 2033)
2. **Catch-up Contributions**: Must be Roth for high earners (> $145K)
3. **529 to Roth IRA Rollovers**: Up to $35K lifetime (subject to conditions)
4. **Emergency Savings**: Employers can match student loan payments to 401(k)

### Inflation Reduction Act (2022)
1. **Corporate AMT**: 15% on book income for large corporations (> $1B avg income)
2. **Stock Buyback Tax**: 1% excise tax on repurchases
3. **Clean Energy Credits**: Expanded for EVs, solar, energy efficiency

### CARES Act / COVID-19 Relief (2020-2021) - Phased Out
- Employee Retention Credit (ERC): Expired 2021 (2022 for recovery startups)
- PPP Loan Forgiveness: Applications closed 2021

### Upcoming Changes (Proposed/Scheduled)

   - 2024: 60%

2. **TCJA Provisions Expiring 2025** (unless extended):

## 🎓 CPA Qualification Requirements

### Uniform CPA Examination
**Administered by**: AICPA (developed) and NASBA (administered)  
**Sections** (4 exams, 4 hours each):
1. **AUD**: Auditing and Attestation
2. **BEC**: Business Environment and Concepts
3. **FAR**: Financial Accounting and Reporting
4. **REG**: Regulation (tax and business law)

**Passing Score**: 75 (on a scale of 0-99)  
**18-Month Window**: Pass all 4 sections within 18 months

### State Licensure Requirements (Varies by State)
**Typical Requirements**:
- **Education**: 150 semester hours (bachelor's + 30 hours typically)
- **Experience**: 1-2 years under licensed CPA
- **Ethics Exam**: AICPA ethics exam or state-specific
- **Residency**: Some states require state residency/employment

**Reciprocity**: Most states have agreements for CPA mobility

### Continuing Professional Education (CPE)
**Requirement**: 40 hours/year or 120 hours/3 years (varies by state)  
**Ethics**: 2-4 hours typically required  
**Reporting**: To state board of accountancy
