## 📊 Accounting Standards

### Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS)
**Based on**: IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards)  
**Applicable to**: Listed companies, large companies (specified criteria)  
**Mandatory from**: April 1, 2016 (phased implementation)

**Ind AS vs IFRS Differences**:
- Ind AS 115: Revenue recognition (similar to IFRS 15)
- Ind AS 116: Leases (similar to IFRS 16)
- Ind AS 109: Financial instruments (similar to IFRS 9)
- Key difference: Some carve-outs from IFRS

### Previous GAAP (for non-Ind AS companies)
**Accounting Standards (AS 1-32)**
- Still applicable to smaller companies
- Set by ICAI
- Based on older IFRS versions

**Company Types**:
1. **Ind AS companies**: Listed, large unlisted (net worth ≥ ₹250 crore)
2. **AS companies**: SMEs, smaller companies
3. **Schedule III**: Financial statement format (Companies Act, 2013)

## 💼 Business Structures

### 1. Sole Proprietorship
- **Tax ID**: PAN (Permanent Account Number)
- **Registration**: Not required with MCA
- **Liability**: Unlimited personal liability
- **Tax Rate**: As per individual slab (5%-30%)
- **Audit**: Required if turnover > ₹1 crore (retail) or ₹50 lakh (professional)

### 2. Partnership Firm
- **Registration**: With Registrar of Firms (optional)
- **Tax ID**: PAN
- **Liability**: Unlimited joint liability
- **Tax Rate**: 30% flat rate
- **Partners**: Minimum 2, Maximum 50
- **Audit**: Required if turnover > ₹1 crore or income > ₹25 lakh

### 3. Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)
- **Registration**: With MCA (mandatory)
- **LLP ID**: Unique identification number
- **Liability**: Limited to contribution
- **Tax Rate**: 30% flat rate
- **Partners**: Minimum 2, no maximum
- **Audit**: Required if turnover > ₹40 lakh or contribution > ₹25 lakh
- **Annual Filing**: Form 11 (Annual Return), Form 8 (Statement of Accounts)

### 4. Private Limited Company (Pvt. Ltd.)
- **Registration**: With MCA under Companies Act, 2013
- **CIN**: Corporate Identification Number
- **Liability**: Limited to share capital
- **Tax Rate**: 25% (turnover ≤ ₹400 crore) or 30%
- **Shareholders**: Minimum 2, Maximum 200
- **Directors**: Minimum 2, Maximum 15
- **Audit**: Mandatory (by CA)
- **Annual Filing**: Form AOC-4 (Financial Statements), Form MGT-7 (Annual Return)

### 5. Public Limited Company
- **Registration**: With MCA
- **Listing**: Can list on stock exchanges
- **Tax Rate**: 25% or 30%
- **Shareholders**: Minimum 7, no maximum
- **Directors**: Minimum 3, Maximum 15
- **Audit**: Mandatory + quarterly reviews if listed
- **Compliance**: High (SEBI regulations if listed)

### 6. One Person Company (OPC)
- **Introduced**: 2013
- **Shareholders**: 1 (with 1 nominee)
- **Directors**: Minimum 1
- **Tax Rate**: 25% or 30%
- **Audit**: Required if turnover > ₹2 crore or capital > ₹50 lakh

## 💰 Tax System

### Income Tax (Direct Tax)

#### Individual Tax Slabs (FY 2025-26)
**Old Regime**:
- Up to ₹2.5 lakh: Nil
- ₹2.5-5 lakh: 5%
- ₹5-10 lakh: 20%
- Above ₹10 lakh: 30%
- Surcharge: 10% (income ₹50L-1Cr), 15% (₹1-2Cr), 25% (₹2-5Cr), 37% (>₹5Cr)
- Health & Education Cess: 4% on tax + surcharge

**New Regime (Default from FY 2023-24)**:
- Up to ₹3 lakh: Nil
- ₹3-6 lakh: 5%
- ₹6-9 lakh: 10%
- ₹9-12 lakh: 15%
- ₹12-15 lakh: 20%
- Above ₹15 lakh: 30%
- No deductions allowed (80C, 80D, HRA, etc.)
- Rebate: ₹25,000 if income ≤ ₹7 lakh (effectively tax-free)

#### Corporate Tax Rates
**Domestic Companies**:
- Turnover ≤ ₹400 crore: 25%
- Turnover > ₹400 crore: 30%
- New manufacturing companies (opted in): 15% (no deductions)
- Surcharge: 7% (income ₹1-10Cr), 12% (>₹10Cr)
- Health & Education Cess: 4%

**Foreign Companies**: 40% + surcharge + cess

#### Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT)
- Rate: 15% on book profit
- Applicable when tax under normal provisions < MAT
- Credit can be carried forward for 15 years

#### Advance Tax
**Due Dates**:
- June 15: 15% of tax
- September 15: 45% of tax
- December 15: 75% of tax
- March 15: 100% of tax

**Interest**: 1% per month for delay (Section 234B, 234C)

### Goods and Services Tax (GST)

#### GST Structure
**Implemented**: July 1, 2017  
**Type**: Destination-based, dual GST

**Components**:
1. **CGST**: Central GST (collected by Central Govt)
2. **SGST**: State GST (collected by State Govt)
3. **IGST**: Integrated GST (for inter-state transactions)
4. **UTGST**: Union Territory GST

#### GST Rates (2025)
- **0%**: Essential items (grains, fresh vegetables, healthcare)
- **5%**: Basic necessities (sugar, tea, coffee, edible oil, coal)
- **12%**: Processed food, computers, mobile phones (below ₹25K)
- **18%**: Standard rate (most goods and services, IT services, capital goods)
- **28%**: Luxury items, sin goods (cars, tobacco, aerated drinks)

#### GST Registration
**Threshold**:
- Goods: ₹40 lakh turnover (₹20 lakh for special category states)
- Services: ₹20 lakh turnover (₹10 lakh for special category states)
- E-commerce sellers: ₹0 (mandatory registration)

**GSTIN**: 15-digit unique identification number (state code + PAN + entity code + check digit)

#### GST Returns
**Monthly**:
- GSTR-1: Outward supplies (11th of next month)
- GSTR-3B: Summary return (20th of next month)

**Annual**:
- GSTR-9: Annual return (December 31)
- GSTR-9C: Reconciliation statement (if turnover > ₹5 crore) - CA certified

**Quarterly** (for small taxpayers - QRMP scheme):
- Turnover < ₹5 crore: Quarterly GSTR-1 + GSTR-3B

#### Input Tax Credit (ITC)
- Can claim credit of GST paid on inputs
- ITC available only on business use
- Reversals required for personal use
- Blocked credits: Motor vehicles (except for specified use), food & beverages

### TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)
**Common TDS Sections**:
- **192**: Salary (as per slab)
- **194A**: Interest (10% if > ₹40K for senior citizens, ₹50K others)
- **194C**: Contractor payments (1% for individuals, 2% for companies)
- **194H**: Commission/brokerage (5%)
- **194I**: Rent (10% for land/building, 2% for equipment)
- **194J**: Professional fees (10%)
- **194Q**: Purchase of goods (0.1% if > ₹50 lakh)

**TDS Returns**:
- Quarterly filing (Q1: July 31, Q2: Oct 31, Q3: Jan 31, Q4: May 31)
- Form 24Q (salary), 26Q (non-salary), 27Q (non-residents)

### Other Taxes
**Professional Tax**: State-level (max ₹2,500/year) - varies by state  
**Property Tax**: Municipal tax on property  
**Stamp Duty**: On property transactions, registration (state-specific)  
**Securities Transaction Tax (STT)**: On stock market transactions

## 🔍 Audit Framework

### Auditing Standards (SAs)
**Issued by**: ICAI - Auditing and Assurance Standards Board (AASB)  
**Based on**: International Standards on Auditing (ISAs)

**Key Standards**:
- **SA 200**: Overall Objectives of Independent Auditor
- **SA 315**: Identifying and Assessing Risks of Material Misstatement
- **SA 330**: Auditor's Responses to Assessed Risks
- **SA 500**: Audit Evidence
- **SA 570**: Going Concern
- **SA 700**: Forming Opinion and Reporting on Financial Statements
- **SA 701**: Communicating Key Audit Matters
- **SA 720**: Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Other Information

### Audit Report Types
1. **Unqualified (Clean) Opinion**: No material misstatements
2. **Qualified Opinion**: Material but not pervasive issues (Except for...)
3. **Adverse Opinion**: Material and pervasive misstatements
4. **Disclaimer of Opinion**: Unable to obtain sufficient evidence

### Special Audits
**Tax Audit (Section 44AB)**:
- Required for businesses exceeding turnover thresholds
- Form 3CA/3CB: Audit report
- Form 3CD: Audit particulars (detailed checklist)
- Due: September 30

**Cost Audit**:
- For specified industries (pharma, chemicals, steel, etc.)
- Required if turnover > ₹35 crore + production > threshold
- Form CRA-1, CRA-2, CRA-3

**Secretarial Audit**:
- Listed companies + unlisted with paid-up capital ≥ ₹50 crore
- By Company Secretary in practice
- Form MR-3 (Secretarial Audit Report)

## 📝 Common Compliance Issues

### Top 10 Issues in India

1. **GST Compliance**
   - Late filing penalties: ₹100/day (max ₹5,000)
   - Interest on late payment: 18% p.a.
   - Matching of ITC (2A vs 3B reconciliation)

2. **TDS Compliance**
   - Late filing fees: ₹200/day
   - Late payment interest: 1-1.5% per month
   - Lower/nil TDS certificate validity

3. **MCA Annual Filings**
   - Late filing fees for companies: ₹100/day (doubles after 3 months)
   - Director penalties: ₹1,000/day
   - Company strike-off risk

4. **Transfer Pricing**
   - Applicable if international/related party transactions
   - Form 3CEB required if transactions > ₹1 crore
   - ALP (Arm's Length Price) determination

5. **FEMA Compliance**
   - Foreign Exchange Management Act
   - Reporting for foreign investments, ECB, ODI
   - RBI penalties for non-compliance

6. **PF/ESIC Compliance**
   - Provident Fund (EPF): 12% employer + 12% employee
   - ESIC: Medical insurance (applicable if wages ≤ ₹21,000)
   - Monthly filing (ECR)

7. **Advance Tax**
   - Interest for shortfall/late payment
   - Correct estimation required

8. **ITR Filing**
   - Late filing fees: ₹5,000 (₹1,000 if income < ₹5L)
   - Loss of refund claim if filed > 1 year late
   - Prosecution for willful default

9. **Depreciation Claims**
   - Different rates for IT vs Companies Act
   - Accelerated depreciation for specific assets
   - Goodwill amortization not allowed for tax

10. **Input Tax Credit Reversals**
    - Personal use reversal required
    - Capital goods ITC spread over 5 years
    - Blocked credits (motor vehicles, etc.)

## 💡 Tax Planning Strategies (Legal)

### For Individuals
1. **Section 80C** (up to ₹1.5 lakh): ELSS, PPF, EPF, NSC, Life Insurance, Home Loan Principal
2. **Section 80D** (up to ₹25K/₹50K): Health insurance premiums
3. **Section 80CCD(1B)** (₹50K): NPS additional deduction
4. **Section 24(b)**: Home loan interest deduction (₹2 lakh)
5. **HRA Exemption**: If living in rented house (not in new regime)
6. **LTA**: Leave Travel Allowance (2 journeys in 4 years)
7. **NPS Tax Benefit**: 40% withdrawal tax-free at maturity

### For Businesses
1. **Section 80-IAC**: 100% deduction for eligible startups (3 years)
2. **Section 80-IBA**: 100% deduction for affordable housing projects
3. **Section 35(2AB)**: 150% deduction for R&D expenditure
4. **Depreciation**: Accelerated depreciation for solar plants, electric vehicles
5. **MAT Credit**: Utilize MAT credit when regular tax > MAT
6. **Carry Forward Losses**: Business loss - 8 years, Capital loss - 8 years, Unabsorbed depreciation - indefinitely
7. **Capital Gains**: Reinvestment exemptions (54, 54EC, 54F)
8. **SEZ Units**: Tax holidays for Special Economic Zone units

## 🎓 Qualification Requirements

### To Practice as CA in India
**Qualification**: Chartered Accountant (CA) from ICAI  
**Duration**: 4.5-5 years  
**Levels**:
1. CPT (Common Proficiency Test) - Discontinued, replaced by Foundation
2. IPCC/Intermediate (8 papers)
3. Articleship (3 years practical training)
4. Final (8 papers)
5. Membership with ICAI

**Can Sign Audit Reports**: Yes  
**Peer Review**: Mandatory for audit firms

### Other Professionals
- **Company Secretary (CS)**: ICSI - for secretarial compliance
- **Cost Accountant (CMA)**: ICMAI - for cost audits
- **Tax Practitioners**: Can file returns but not audit

## 🚩 Red Flags for AI Responses

### High-Risk Topics (Require Human Review)
1. **Transfer Pricing**: Complex ALP calculations
2. **International Taxation**: DTAA interpretation, BEPS
3. **Indirect Tax**: GST classification disputes, ITC eligibility
4. **FEMA**: Foreign investment structuring
5. **Black Money Act**: Foreign asset disclosure
6. **Benami Transactions**: Beneficial ownership issues

### Always Cite Specific Sections
- Income Tax Act: "Section 80C" not just "80C"
- GST Act: "Section 16 of CGST Act" with exact wording
- Companies Act: "Section 139 of Companies Act, 2013"

### Jurisdiction Clarifications
- **Central vs State**: GST has dual levy, specify CGST+SGST or IGST
- **State-specific**: Professional tax, stamp duty vary by state
- **City-specific**: Property tax, municipal compliances
